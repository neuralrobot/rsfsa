Fire Safety Equipment

Fire Blanket Safety

Essential fire safety equipment for vehicles and homes. Learn how to use fire blankets effectively to suppress small fires and protect lives.

What is a Fire Blanket?

A fire blanket is a safety device designed to extinguish incipient (starting) fires by smothering them. It consists of fire-resistant materials that cut off the oxygen supply to the fire, effectively suppressing it before it can spread.

Critical for Vehicle Safety

Fire blankets are particularly important for vehicle safety as they can quickly suppress small engine fires, electrical fires, or clothing fires. They're easy to use, require no training, and don't leave residue like chemical extinguishers.

Unlike fire extinguishers, fire blankets don't require maintenance, don't expire (when stored properly), and can be used multiple times if not damaged. They're also safe to use on electrical fires without the risk of electric shock.

How to Use a Fire Blanket

1
Turn off heat source

If possible, turn off the heat source (stove, engine, etc.) before deploying the fire blanket.

2
Pull down sharply

Grab the tabs and pull the fire blanket down sharply to release it from its container.

3
Protect your hands

Wrap the corners around your hands to protect them from heat and flames.

4
Place over fire

Carefully place the blanket over the fire, starting from the near edge and working away.

5
Leave in place

Leave the blanket in place for at least 15 minutes to ensure the fire is completely extinguished.

6
Monitor area

Monitor the area for re-ignition and have a backup fire suppression method ready.

Safety Tips and Best Practices

✓ Do's
  • Keep fire blankets in easily accessible locations
  • Check the blanket annually for damage
  • Use on small fires only (less than 1 meter)
  • Turn off heat source before using if possible
  • Call emergency services for larger fires
  • Have an escape route planned
✗ Don'ts
  • Don't use on large or spreading fires
  • Don't remove immediately after deployment
  • Don't use on person unless trained
  • Don't attempt to fight fire if escape route blocked
  • Don't reuse damaged blankets
  • Don't store in damp conditions

When NOT to Use a Fire Blanket

Do not use fire blankets on large fires, fires involving gas under pressure, electrical installations above 1000V, or when the fire involves volatile substances like petrol. In these cases, evacuate immediately and call emergency services.

Fire Safety Saves Lives

Every vehicle and home should have proper fire safety equipment. Fire blankets are an essential part of your emergency preparedness.
