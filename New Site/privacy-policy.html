1. Introduction

The Road Safety Foundation of South Africa ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, use our services, or interact with us in any capacity.

This policy is designed to comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) and other applicable South African privacy laws. By using our website or services, you consent to the practices described in this policy.

POPIA Compliance

We are committed to complying with POPIA and ensuring that your personal information is processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently. We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, postal address
  • WhatsApp Subscription Data: WhatsApp number, preferred traffic routes, communication preferences
  • Website Usage Data: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, time spent on pages
  • Communication Records: Correspondence with us via email, phone, or contact forms
  • Newsletter Subscriptions: Email address and communication preferences
  • Event Participation: Information provided when registering for road safety events or programs
  • Feedback and Surveys: Responses to surveys, feedback forms, or research participation

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect:

  • Log data (IP address, browser type, operating system)
  • Device information (device type, screen resolution, operating system version)
  • Usage analytics (pages viewed, time spent, click patterns)
  • Location data (general geographic location based on IP address)
  • Referral information (websites that directed you to our site)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

3.1 Core Services

  • Providing road safety information and educational content
  • Sending traffic alerts and updates via WhatsApp (with your consent)
  • Responding to your inquiries and providing customer support
  • Processing newsletter subscriptions and sending relevant updates
  • Organizing and managing road safety events and programs

3.2 Website Improvement

  • Analyzing website usage to improve user experience
  • Troubleshooting technical issues
  • Enhancing website security and preventing fraud
  • Conducting research to improve our services

3.3 Communication

  • Sending important updates about our services
  • Providing information about road safety initiatives
  • Sharing relevant traffic and safety alerts
  • Inviting you to participate in surveys or feedback sessions

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

Under POPIA, we process your personal information based on the following lawful grounds:

  • Consent: When you have given clear, informed consent for specific processing activities (e.g., WhatsApp alerts, newsletters)
  • Legitimate Interests: When processing is necessary for our legitimate interests in promoting road safety and providing educational content
  • Public Interest: When processing supports our mission of improving road safety, which serves the public interest
  • Compliance with Legal Obligations: When required to comply with applicable South African laws and regulations
  • Performance of Contract: When necessary to fulfill services you have requested from us

Your Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we rely on your consent, you have the right to withdraw it at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

5. Sharing Your Information

We may share your personal information in the following circumstances:

5.1 Service Providers

  • WhatsApp Business API providers for sending traffic alerts
  • Email service providers for newsletters and communications
  • Website hosting and analytics providers
  • IT support and security service providers

5.2 Partner Organizations

  • Government agencies and traffic authorities (for road safety initiatives)
  • Educational institutions (for road safety programs)
  • Corporate partners (for joint safety campaigns)
  • Other road safety organizations (for collaborative projects)

5.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information when required by law or to:

  • Comply with court orders, subpoenas, or legal processes
  • Protect our rights, property, or safety
  • Investigate potential violations of our terms of service
  • Cooperate with law enforcement agencies

Data Processing Agreements

All third parties who process personal information on our behalf are bound by data processing agreements that ensure they maintain the same level of data protection as required by POPIA.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information:

6.1 Technical Measures

  • SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • Secure server infrastructure and regular security updates
  • Access controls and authentication systems
  • Regular security monitoring and vulnerability assessments
  • Data backup and recovery procedures

6.2 Organizational Measures

  • Staff training on data protection and privacy
  • Limited access to personal information on a need-to-know basis
  • Regular review and updating of security policies
  • Incident response procedures for data breaches
  • Data protection impact assessments for new processing activities

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to implementing industry best practices.

7. Your Rights Under POPIA

Under POPIA, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

7.1 Right of Access

You have the right to request confirmation of whether we process your personal information and to obtain a copy of that information.

7.2 Right to Correction

You can request that we correct or update inaccurate or incomplete personal information.

7.3 Right to Erasure

You can request that we delete your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when it's no longer necessary for the original purpose.

7.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You can request that we limit how we use your personal information in certain situations.

7.5 Right to Object

You can object to the processing of your personal information for direct marketing purposes or where we rely on legitimate interests.

7.6 Right to Data Portability

You can request a copy of your personal information in a structured, commonly used format.

Response Time

We will respond to your requests within 30 days as required by POPIA. If we need additional time, we will inform you of the reason for the delay.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected:

  • WhatsApp Subscriptions: Until you unsubscribe or request deletion
  • Newsletter Subscriptions: Until you unsubscribe
  • Contact Inquiries: For 2 years after the last communication
  • Website Analytics: For 2 years from collection
  • Event Participation: For 3 years for program evaluation purposes
  • Legal Requirements: As required by applicable South African laws

After the retention period expires, we will securely delete or anonymize your personal information unless we are required to retain it for legal or regulatory purposes.

9. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience:

9.1 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for basic website functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences
  • Marketing Cookies: Used to deliver relevant content and advertisements

9.2 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies from being set

10. Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not directed to children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

For our road safety education programs in schools, we obtain appropriate consent from parents or guardians before collecting any personal information from minors.

11. International Data Transfers

Some of our service providers may be located outside of South Africa. When we transfer your personal information internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Adequacy decisions by the Information Regulator of South Africa
  • Standard contractual clauses approved by relevant authorities
  • Binding corporate rules for multinational companies
  • Certification schemes and codes of conduct

We will only transfer your personal information to countries or organizations that provide adequate protection for personal information as required by POPIA.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the bottom of this page
  • Notify you via email if you have subscribed to our communications
  • Post a notice on our website for significant changes
  • Obtain your consent for material changes that affect your rights

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your privacy.
