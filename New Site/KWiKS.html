Community Impact Initiative

KWiKS

Keep Warm, Keep Safe

Protecting South Africa's most vulnerable road users - rural school children who walk long distances to school in harsh weather conditions.

Make a Donation Learn More

Our Mission

Every day, thousands of children in rural South Africa walk to school in dangerous weather conditions - from torrential rain to freezing temperatures. Many lack proper protection, making them vulnerable to hypothermia, illness, and accidents. KWiKS provides life-saving poncho blankets and highly reflective raincoats to keep these brave children warm, dry, and visible to motorists.

The Challenge We Face

Extreme Weather Exposure

Children walk up to 10km daily in temperatures as low as -5°C, with no adequate protection from rain, snow, or freezing winds.

Poor Visibility

Dark clothing and poor weather conditions make children nearly invisible to drivers, especially during early morning and late afternoon commutes.

Health Risks

Prolonged exposure leads to respiratory infections, hypothermia, and compromised immune systems affecting school attendance and academic performance.

KWiKS Protection in Action

Keeping children warm, dry, and visible on their way to school

Our Solution: KWiKS Protection Kit

Each KWiKS kit provides comprehensive protection designed specifically for walking to school in harsh weather conditions.

Thermal Poncho Blankets

Water-resistant, insulated poncho blankets that provide warmth and protection from rain and wind. Lightweight and easy to carry, designed for children aged 6-18.

  • Waterproof outer layer
  • Thermal insulation
  • Wind-resistant material
  • Compact and lightweight

Reflective Rain Coats

High-visibility raincoats with strategically placed reflective strips that make children visible from up to 500 meters away, even in poor weather conditions.

  • 360° reflective strips
  • Bright fluorescent colors
  • Fully waterproof
  • Durable construction

Safety Education

Along with protective gear, we provide road safety education sessions teaching children how to stay safe while walking to school.

  • Road safety workshops
  • Weather awareness training
  • Proper gear usage
  • Emergency procedures

KWiKS Journey

2019 - Initiative Launch

KWiKS program launched in response to growing concerns about child pedestrian safety in rural areas. First distribution of 500 protection kits in the Eastern Cape.

2020 - Rapid Expansion

Program expanded to 3 provinces. Partnered with local schools and community leaders. Distributed 2,500 kits and established sustainable distribution networks.

2021 - Technology Integration

Introduced GPS tracking for high-risk areas and mobile app for reporting weather-related incidents. Enhanced reflective technology in raincoats.

2022 - Community Partnerships

Established partnerships with 200+ schools and local governments. Launched train-the-trainer program for educators.

2023 - National Recognition

KWiKS received national recognition for child safety innovation. Reached milestone of 10,000 children protected across 5 provinces.

2024 - Ongoing Impact

Current focus on reaching 20,000 children by end of 2024. Expanding to include winter gear and improving sustainability of materials used.
