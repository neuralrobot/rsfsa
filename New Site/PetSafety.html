Pet Safety in Vehicles

Protecting your furry family members during vehicle travel through proper restraints, secure carriers, and safe travel practices.

Keeping Your Pets Safe

Essential equipment and practices for pet safety during vehicle travel.

Pet Restraint Systems

Essential safety harnesses that work with existing seatbelts to protect pets and passengers during travel, emergency stops, or collisions.

A properly fitted pet harness prevents your pet from becoming a projectile during sudden stops, protecting both your pet and all passengers.
Travel Carriers & Crates

Secure carriers and crates provide safe environments for smaller pets, properly secured to prevent movement during sudden stops or accidents.

Well-ventilated carriers should be secured with seatbelts and positioned where your pet can see you, reducing travel anxiety.
Proper Installation

Correct installation and usage of pet restraints is crucial for maximum protection during travel, emergency braking, and collision scenarios.

Always test restraint systems before traveling and ensure your pet is comfortable through gradual introduction.

Pet Travel Safety Guidelines

Why Pet Restraints Matter

Pets are often considered family members and travel with us in our vehicles. As with children or anything else not safely secured, they too can be injured or cause injury to other passengers in the vehicle. Unrestrained pets can become dangerous projectiles during sudden stops or collisions.

Choosing the Right Restraint
  • For Large Dogs: Use crash-tested harnesses that attach to existing seatbelt systems
  • For Small Dogs & Cats: Secure carriers or travel crates that can be strapped in place
  • Size Matters: Ensure proper fit - restraints should be snug but comfortable
  • Test First: Introduce restraints gradually to reduce pet anxiety
Never Allow Unrestrained Pets

An unrestrained 60-pound dog in a car traveling 35 mph can exert 2,700 pounds of force in a collision - enough to seriously injure or kill passengers. Never allow pets to ride in the front seat, stick their heads out windows, or roam freely in the vehicle.

Travel Preparation Tips
  • Take short practice trips to get your pet used to restraints
  • Never leave pets unattended in vehicles, especially in hot weather
  • Bring water, favorite toys, and familiar blankets for comfort
  • Plan regular stops for bathroom breaks and exercise
  • Keep vaccination records and ID tags current
