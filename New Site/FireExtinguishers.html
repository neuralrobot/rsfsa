Extinguisher Types & Applications

Water Extinguishers

‚úì Effective Against:
  • Class A fires only
  • Paper, wood, fabric
  • Interior upholstery
‚úó Never Use On:
  • Electrical fires
  • Fuel/oil fires
  • Grease fires
Vehicle Use: Limited application. Mainly for interior fires when electrical power is disconnected.

CO‚ÇÇ Extinguishers

‚úì Effective Against:
  • Class B & C fires
  • Electrical equipment
  • Fuel fires
‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations:
  • Can re-ignite
  • No cooling effect
  • Displaces oxygen
Vehicle Use: Excellent for engine bay electrical fires. Clean, no residue.

Dry Chemical (ABC)

‚úì Effective Against:
  • Class A, B, C fires
  • Most versatile
  • Quick knockdown
‚ö†Ô∏è Considerations:
  • Corrosive residue
  • Can damage electronics
  • Messy cleanup
Vehicle Use: Most recommended for general vehicle use. Handles multiple fire types.

Foam Extinguishers

‚úì Effective Against:
  • Class A & B fires
  • Fuel spill fires
  • Oil/grease fires
‚úó Never Use On:
  • Electrical fires
  • Class C fires
  • Live electrical equipment
Vehicle Use: Good for fuel fires. Forms barrier to prevent re-ignition.

Vehicle-Specific Requirements

Passenger Vehicle Requirements

Recommended Setup
  • Type: 2kg ABC Dry Chemical
  • Location: Driver's side, accessible
  • Mounting: Secure bracket system
  • Backup: Fire blanket for small fires
Best Mounting Locations
  • Under driver's seat (if space)
  • Boot/trunk (secured)
  • Behind driver's seat
  • Door panel mount (SUVs)
SA Legal Requirements
  • Not mandatory for private cars
  • Highly recommended
  • Insurance benefits possible
  • Fleet policies may require
Cost Considerations
  • 2kg ABC: R200-400
  • Mounting bracket: R50-100
  • Annual service: R100-200
  • Much cheaper than vehicle loss
Quick Access Guide
üöó Sedan/Hatchback

üöô SUV/4x4

üöê MPV/Van

Remember: Seconds count in a fire. Practice accessing your extinguisher.

Early Warning Signs

Smell
  • Burning plastic
  • Hot oil/rubber
  • Electrical burning
  • Fuel vapors
Visual
  • Smoke from hood
  • Steam/vapor
  • Sparks/arcing
  • Glowing components
Temperature
  • Engine overheating
  • Hot floor/pedals
  • Burning dashboard
  • Excessive heat
Sounds
  • Electrical crackling
  • Hissing/popping
  • Engine knocking
  • Unusual noises
Action: If you notice any of these signs, pull over immediately and investigate safely. Early detection saves lives and property.
